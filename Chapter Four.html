Chapter Four

The Princess

Prince Alex found himself so stunned, he could not form a reaction for several seconds. The white knight glanced back and forth between himself and Sir David, who seemed to be in an equal state of shock.

Who…who are you, Prince Alex asked when he finally gathered his bearings. High above, the dragon snorted and shook its massive head. Its shining orange eyes watched him intently.

My name is Kaya. Who are you and why were you trying to kill my dragon?

Your dragon? Sir David asked, finally pulling himself from the dirt. M’lady, I think you are mistaken. This dragon’s greed sent it to attack a nearby kingdom. He kidnapped a princess!

The knight let out a sharp laugh.

Perhaps I should reintroduce myself. I am Princess Kaya from the land of Dayn. And as for the greed of this dragon, SHE is nothing short of noble and would never act on such petty human desires. Besides, I wasn’t kidnapped. I ran away to join the riders.

The Riders of the North Wind?

The very same, the princess said with a nod. The great dragon lowered her head\ to stare at Sir David. Alex suddenly felt a surge of protectiveness, but it was soon buried under his incredulity.

M’lady, I still don’t understand. If you went off to join the riders, why would your father have made up such a tale? Why would he have begged us this dragon if it was yours?

The princess sighed and sheathed her sword.

My father wants Nikkia dead. He believes she will turn me against him and lead to the downfall of his kingdom. He doesn’t understand that it is he himself who has turned me against him.

She stared into the distance. It wasn’t until Alex cleared his throat that she refocused on the Prince.

Anyway, as you can see, there’s no reason for you to do anything so you might as well return home.

I quite agree, your majesty. Though perhaps you could do a quick favor for us before you continue North… Prince Alex said, his silvery words slipping off his honey dressed tongue.
