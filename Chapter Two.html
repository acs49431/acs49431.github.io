Chapter TWO

The Knight

Two days after the dragon attacked, a messenger came sprinting into the great hall of the Castle, causing a great disturbance. Alex, his brothers, and many of the inhabiting knights gathered to listen to the courier’s message. The messenger spoke of a fierce and terrible dragon who had taken the princess of the small land of Dayn and presented a message from their king, begging the king of Solaria for help.

Immediately realizing the threat of having a dragon on nearby lands, the King of Solaria agreed to help and called forth his greatest knight, Sir David, to slay the dragon and bring back the princess safe and sound. Realizing this would perhaps be his only chance to own a kingdom of his own, Prince Alex rushed forward and volunteered to accompany the knight on the journey. His father said no but with a few persuasive words, Alex was soon allowed to undertake the task.

He and Sir David set off in the direction of the white hills, where the dragon was rumored to have made its hunting grounds. The journey took several days and nights and all the while, Sir David and Prince Alex talked constantly about their lives. Alex told David about growing up as the youngest son of the King, spending hours and hours reading around the castle, and learning how to talk his way out of precarious situations. David told Alex about his childhood as lower nobility, about fighting with sticks in the woods to practice his swordsmanship, and about the first beast he had ever slain. By the time the two had reached the base of the hills, they had become close friends.

Leaving their horses behind, Sir David and Prince Alex began to ascend the white hills. They traveled for hours until finally, with weary legs and drooping heads, the two settled into a small crevice on one of the hillsides. They lit their fire, cooked their food, and settled in for the night. It wasn’t until he was looking out over the expanse of hills that Alex realized one was moving.
